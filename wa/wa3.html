Overview

Earth is our home planet and the only known place in the universe confirmed to host life. With a vast range of biodiversity, the Earth's surface is mainly covered with water and is surrounded by a life-supporting atmosphere.

Physical Characteristics

The Earth is the fifth-largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter of approximately 12,742 km. It features a solid and active surface with mountains, valleys, and oceans. Earth's atmosphere is rich in nitrogen and oxygen, which is critical for life as we know it.

Orbit and Rotation

The Earth orbits the Sun once every 365.25 days, which defines our year. It has a tilted axis of rotation, resulting in seasonal changes throughout the year. The planet rotates once every 24 hours, creating the day-night cycle.

Habitat

Earth's environment is suitable for a diverse range of ecosystems, from arid deserts to lush rainforests. It's the interactions between the Earth's land, ocean, atmosphere, and life that make it uniquely capable of supporting life.
